### 4.4. INTELLIGENCE LOGIC IMPROVEMENTS

*   *Debate Engine Tweaks*:
    *   *Structured Argumentation*: Implement a protocol where agents (or agent groups representing archetypes like Warren, Cathie, Quant) present arguments for/against a trade, strategy change, or risk adjustment.
    *   *Evidence-Based Reasoning*: Each argument must be supported by data, historical patterns, or logical inference.
    *   *Counter-Argument Generation*: Agents are tasked with generating counter-arguments to proposed actions, forcing a more thorough analysis of potential downsides.
    *   *Consensus Building*: A weighted consensus mechanism (see DAO-like voting) determines the final decision.
*   *DAO-like Voting in Agent Layer*:
    *   *Formalized Voting*: For critical decisions (e.g., capital allocation, new strategy deployment, risk parameter adjustments), agents cast votes.
    *   *Weighted Voting*: Votes are weighted based on:
        *   *Agent Experience*: More experienced agents (higher performance scores, longer tenure) have higher vote weight.
        *   *Confidence in Decision*: Agents' self-assessed confidence in their proposed action.
        *   *Role/Department*: Director-level agents or agents from the Risk Control department might have higher weight on risk-related votes.
        *   *Past Accuracy*: Agents with a higher historical accuracy rate for similar decisions have increased weight.
    *   *Hierarchical Consensus*: Departmental decisions require internal consensus. Cross-departmental decisions (e.g., a major trade) require consensus from relevant departments (Market Intel, Trade Ops, Risk Control). The CEO AI retains final veto power, but its exercise should be logged and justified.
